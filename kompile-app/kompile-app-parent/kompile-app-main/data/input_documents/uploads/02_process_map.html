// 01 · The pain

Where time, accuracy, and trust leak in mid-market FP&A

Six recurring failure modes we observed at Northstar. Customer-specific in detail; structurally identical across the FP&A teams we've talked to.

28h
Manual data wrangling

Three regional forecasts arrive in three different formats — different channel names, different currencies, different SKU codes, even different fiscal calendars. Corporate analysts spend most of their cycle just normalizing.

BD+10
Slow close cycle

Two full business weeks from period-end to projection. By the time the CFO sees the close, the world has moved on. Decisions are made on stale information.

~6
Errors caught per cycle (and how many missed?)

Hardcoded subtotals don't match the rows above them. Channel mismatches go unflagged. Currency tags get dropped. The 6 errors per cycle that are caught are scary; the unknown number missed is scarier.

3 × n
Fragmented, parallel processes

Three regions × N analysts each, with parallel spreadsheets, idiosyncratic templates, and undocumented escalation rules. Knowledge lives in heads. Departures cost institutional memory. Onboarding takes a quarter.

0
Bandwidth for scenarios

By the time the close lands, the team is starting the next one. When leadership asks "what if EUR weakens 3%?" or "what if Target line review goes south?" — answering takes another week. So leadership stops asking.

7
Systems to reconcile each cycle

NetSuite + Shopify + Amazon + Salesforce + Workday + Treasury + Excel master. Audit prep alone is ~50 hours per quarter, reconstructing a paper trail across all of them.

Not RPA. Not Zapier. RPA automates clicks. KAA automates the understanding behind the clicks. RPA breaks when an Excel column moves; KAA's ontology re-binds. RPA can't tell you why a rule exists; KAA links every rule to source evidence — manual section, screen recording, interview turn.

Living architecture, not a SaaS dependency

What you keep when ProssimoTech walks out: an evidence graph, a semantic ontology, a versioned process model, and agent specs with their eval sets. All editable. All auditable. All yours. Procedure

Compute ratio = (sum of stage 4+ pipeline closing in next 3 months) / (forecast wholesale revenue in next 3 months), per region. Compare to the historical band (1.4× – 2.2×).

Methodology and assumptions

Stochastic model: Each metric assumes monthly geometric Brownian motion. Expected return (drift) and volatility (σ) are estimated from Northstar's trailing 36-month data and industry benchmarks. Inter-metric correlations are treated as independent for simplicity; production deployments add a correlation matrix for improved precision.

Simulation scale: Each metric × each month × 1,000 independent trials = ~180,000 samples. From these, the P10 / P25 / P50 / P75 / P90 percentiles are extracted. The bands widen with time to visualize compounding uncertainty.

Use cases: Downside risk assessment in investment decisions, stress testing in operating plans, multi-scenario comparison, and ROI distributions for SaaS investments. In production, the agent ingests monthly actuals and continuously re-estimates drift and σ.

// What you're looking at

Every business has a vocabulary. Semantic alignment is the discipline of making that vocabulary agree across silos — and the ontology is the artifact it produces.

At Northstar, three regional sales teams use eleven different spellings of "DTC" — DTC, Direct-to-Consumer, ecom, Direct, EC, Marketplace, plus regional dialects. Three currencies arrive in three different magnitudes (JPY thousands, AUD millions, SGD actuals). One product is RST-001 in corporate, NS-UK-RestBath16 in EMEA, and JP-RST-001 in Japan. None of this is wrong — every region has reasons. But none of it can be summed, compared, or projected without a translation layer.

Semantic alignment is the ongoing discipline that produces and maintains that translation layer. Its output — the ontology (or semantic layer in data-architecture parlance) — is a catalog of every concept in the close cycle, with controlled vocabularies, validation rules, relationships, and full provenance back to the manuals, macros, recordings, and interviews where each rule was learned. Agents read from it; humans edit it; the customer keeps it.
