// 01 · The pain

Where time, accuracy, and trust leak in mid-market FP&A

Six recurring failure modes we observed at Northstar. Customer-specific in detail; structurally identical across the FP&A teams we've talked to.

28h
Manual data wrangling

Three regional forecasts arrive in three different formats — different channel names, different currencies, different SKU codes, even different fiscal calendars. Corporate analysts spend most of their cycle just normalizing.

BD+10
Slow close cycle

Two full business weeks from period-end to projection. By the time the CFO sees the close, the world has moved on. Decisions are made on stale information.

~6
Errors caught per cycle (and how many missed?)

Hardcoded subtotals don't match the rows above them. Channel mismatches go unflagged. Currency tags get dropped. The 6 errors per cycle that are caught are scary; the unknown number missed is scarier.

3 × n
Fragmented, parallel processes

Three regions × N analysts each, with parallel spreadsheets, idiosyncratic templates, and undocumented escalation rules. Knowledge lives in heads. Departures cost institutional memory. Onboarding takes a quarter.

0
Bandwidth for scenarios

By the time the close lands, the team is starting the next one. When leadership asks "what if EUR weakens 3%?" or "what if Target line review goes south?" — answering takes another week. So leadership stops asking.

7
Systems to reconcile each cycle

NetSuite + Shopify + Amazon + Salesforce + Workday + Treasury + Excel master. Audit prep alone is ~50 hours per quarter, reconstructing a paper trail across all of them.

// 02 · The methodology

KAA — Knowledge and Action Automation

Not RPA. Not Zapier. ProssimoTech's six-layer methodology mines the customer's process via multi-modal observation, extracts a domain ontology with semantic alignment across regional finance data, codifies it into a governed process model, and then runs it with agentic workflows on top of integrations. The customer keeps every artifact — decision-grade by design.

Why this isn't RPA

RPA automates clicks. KAA automates the understanding behind the clicks. RPA breaks when an Excel column moves; KAA's ontology re-binds. RPA can't tell you why a rule exists; KAA links every rule to source evidence — manual section, screen recording, interview turn.

Living architecture, not a SaaS dependency

What you keep when ProssimoTech walks out: an evidence graph, a semantic ontology, a versioned process model, and agent specs with their eval sets. All editable. All auditable. All yours. Every cycle's telemetry refines the ontology automatically — the enterprise intelligence infrastructure gets better at your business over time, with or without us in the room.

Phase 2

Validate & reconcile

Day 5–6. Variance triage on each regional submission, cross-check forecast against pipeline, FX-translate to USD.

Step 2.1
Validate forecast workbook & triage variances
Tie checks · pattern triage · auto-fix or escalate.
Analyst ▼

Tie checks

  • D60 (regional total) = sum(Detail tab) — tolerance $1,000
  • Channel total = sum(SKU level) within each channel
  • Currency tags consistent on every revenue line

Variance triage rules

  • Pattern: miscoded SKU → auto-correct in place, log on Adjustments tab
  • Pattern: channel mismatch → always escalate (per M. Chen)
  • Pattern: currency tag mismatch → auto-correct, notify regional lead
  • Pattern: calendar shift → auto-correct using master calendar
  • Other / unknown → return to regional lead with variance log
Audit trail: every correction recorded with before/after values, source evidence, and approval status.
Step 2.2
Reconcile forecast to Salesforce pipeline
Pipeline coverage ratio for wholesale forecast.
Control ▼

Procedure

Compute ratio = (sum of stage 4+ pipeline closing in next 3 months) / (forecast wholesale revenue in next 3 months), per region. Compare to the historical band (1.4× – 2.2×).

  • Within band → pass
  • Below band (forecast too high relative to pipeline) → flag, request commentary from regional lead
  • Above band (forecast conservative relative to pipeline) → flag, request commentary
  • Stale deals (no activity > 30 days) excluded from numerator per D. Singh
Step 2.3
FX-translate forecast lines to USD
Apply forward curve · honor hedge rates where applicable.
System ▼

Procedure

For each non-USD line: lookup currency tag → join to FX Forward Curve at the appropriate horizon (M+1 forward for next month, M+2 for two months out, M+3 for three). For hedged exposures: apply hedge rate from the treasury hedging schedule, with CTA booked to OCI.

Validation

  • No translated row may have rate = 1.0 unless currency = USD
  • USD totals must reconcile to local-currency × rate within $0.01
Phase 3

Project & consolidate

Day 7–9. Project COGS and OpEx; consolidate group P&L with intercompany eliminations.

Step 3.1
Project COGS at standard cost
Standard cost rates × forecast volumes; layer in known cost changes.
Analyst ▼

Procedure

Apply COGS_Standards_FY26.xlsx rates by SKU to forecast unit volumes. Layer in known cost changes from procurement (e.g., a 4% increase in glass packaging effective May). Compute purchase price variance (PPV) reserve based on commodity hedging cover.

Cross-checks

  • Implied gross margin per channel within ±150bps of trailing 6 months
  • Total projected COGS within 5% of run-rate adjusted for volume
Step 3.2
Roll forward OpEx and headcount comp
Run-rate + step changes + Workday-driven comp.
Analyst ▼

Procedure

Take month-3 OpEx run-rate, layer in any known step changes (e.g., a new lease commencing), apply headcount plan from Workday with comp rates and benefits load, add discretionary spend per the marketing calendar.

Open question: Two analysts apply different attrition assumptions (4% vs. 6% annualized). The agent will use 4% as the documented HRIS plan, but flag for human confirmation.
Step 3.3
Consolidate & eliminate intercompany
Group P&L · 3-statement view · intercompany eliminations net to 0.
Analyst ▼

Procedure

Sum entity-level P&Ls. Apply intercompany eliminations from the IC matrix (US-EMEA royalty, US-APAC product transfer, EMEA-APAC services). Verify IC eliminations net to zero ±$1k. Produce group P&L by month for next 3 months.

Manual override: The Apr 2026 cycle showed a $130k manual IC adjustment by Controller — this is being investigated as part of confidence-gap remediation.

Review & publish

Day 10. Variance commentary, Controller and CFO sign-off, publish.

Step 4.1
Variance commentary & CFO pack
Auto-drafted commentary vs. last forecast / budget / prior year.
Analyst ▼

Auto-commentary rules

  • Any line with variance >5% or >$500k vs. last forecast → must have commentary
  • Drill-down evidence cited inline (which forecast row drove which group-level swing)
  • Commentary follows house style: business-cause first, financial impact second, mitigations third

CFO pack contents

  • 1-page executive summary
  • Group P&L by month, 3 months forward
  • Channel breakdown
  • Top 5 swing drivers
  • Variance commentary
  • Risks & opportunities log
Step 4.2
Controller & CFO sign-off
Two-stage approval. Controller for close mechanics, CFO for the projection.
Approval ▼

Approval pattern

  • Controller (L. Okafor): signs off that close mechanics are sound — TB ties, IC nets to zero, all 7 controls passed
  • CFO (M. Sato): signs off that the projection is reasonable — variance commentary stands up, key risks acknowledged
  • If CFO out of office → SVP Finance has step-up authority documented in the BCP plan
  • Both approvals captured with timestamp, comment, and identity-verified signature in the audit log
Step 4.3
Publish & archive
Post to Looker dashboard · email deck to leadership · archive workpapers + audit trail.
System ▼

Procedure

Push group P&L to Looker via the Northstar BI pipeline. Email the CFO Pack PDF + Excel to the standing distribution list. Archive: every input file, intermediate workpaper, the audit-trail JSON, and the final outputs to S3 with 7-year retention.

Audit trail format: machine-readable JSON capturing every step, input hash, output hash, agent decision, human override (if any), approver, timestamp.

The seven controls

Each control fires automatically and writes to the audit trail. Failures gate the next step until cleared by a human or the agent's auto-correction agrees with policy.

C-01 · TB ties = Σ debits = Σ credits in NetSuite TB extract
C-02 · Regional total = |D60 − Σ Detail rows| ≤ $1,000 per regional workbook
C-03 · Pipeline coverage ∈ Pipeline ÷ wholesale forecast ∈ [1.4×, 2.2×] (per region)
C-04 · FX consistency = USD line = local × rate ± $0.01 for every translated row
C-05 · Gross margin band ∈ implied GM ∈ trailing 6mo ± 150bps (per channel) — else commentary required
C-06 · IC eliminations = Σ IC eliminations = 0 ± $1,000
C-07 · Variance threshold → |Δ vs. last forecast| > 5% or > $500k → must have written commentary

Recommended pilot. Start with the highest-leverage, lowest-risk slice: regional forecast ingest, validation, triage, and FX translation (Phases 1–2). This is where the corporate FP&A team currently spends ~70% of their cycle time; the work is highly repetitive and rule-bound; and the human-in-the-loop pattern is well understood.

Out of scope for pilot. Intercompany elimination logic and final CFO sign-off remain fully human until confidence on those steps reaches threshold. This is the safe path for a controlled, auditable rollout.

Risk & control

What we explicitly do not change in the pilot.

What "Day 1" looks like for the corporate FP&A team

After pilot go-live, the analyst's day-of-close looks like this — most of the work is review, not data wrangling.

  1. Open the agent's overnight ingest report (≈ 5 min). All 3 regional forecasts ingested, 12 of 14 variances auto-corrected, 2 escalated.
  2. Review the 2 escalations and either approve or send back to region (≈ 15 min).
  3. Open the FX-translated draft P&L; spot-check the new forward curve impact (≈ 10 min).
  4. Begin Phase 3 (projection) with clean inputs already in place.
